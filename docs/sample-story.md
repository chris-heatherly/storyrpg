# The Midnight Broadcast

## Genre
Supernatural Mystery

## Synopsis
A late-night radio host begins receiving calls from listeners who died years ago. As she investigates, she discovers her station was built on the site of a tragic fire, and the dead have unfinished business only she can help resolve. But someone living wants the secrets of that night to stay buried forever.

## Tone
Atmospheric, suspenseful, with moments of dark humor and genuine heart. Think "Welcome to Night Vale" meets "The Sixth Sense."

## Themes
- truth vs. comfortable lies
- redemption
- the weight of secrets
- connection across impossible divides

## World
A small coastal town called Hollow Bay, where the line between the living and dead has always been thin. The town has a history of strange occurrences that locals dismiss as "just Hollow Bay being Hollow Bay." The radio station KHBY sits on a hill overlooking the town, broadcasting from a converted Victorian mansion.

Time Period: Present day
Technology: Modern, though the radio station uses vintage equipment that seems to work better than it should

## Protagonist
Name: Diana Vance
Description: A burned-out journalist who took the graveyard shift DJ job as a last resort after a scandal ended her investigative reporting career. Skeptical by nature, sharp-tongued, but secretly desperate to believe in something again. Mid-30s, lives alone with a cat named Scoop.
Pronouns: she/her

## Characters
- Marcus Webb: The station's elderly engineer who knows more than he lets on. Worked at KHBY since before the fire. Protective of Diana in a gruff, grandfatherly way.
- Caller 7: The first ghost caller. A teenage girl who died in the 1987 fire. Scared, confused, but determined to deliver a warning.
- Sheriff Tanya Reeves: Local law enforcement who dismisses Diana's concerns. Her family has deep roots in Hollow Bay's power structure.
- Pete Holloway: A true-crime podcaster who arrives in town, claiming to investigate the old fire. His real motives are unclear.

## Locations
- KHBY Radio Station: A creaky Victorian mansion converted to a broadcast studio. The basement is off-limits, sealed since the fire.
- The Hollow Bay Diner: 24-hour greasy spoon where night owls and insomniacs gather. Diana's regular haunt.
- Ashwood Cemetery: Where many fire victims are buried. The graves face away from the radio station hill.
- The Lighthouse: Abandoned but still visible from town. Local legend says its light sometimes turns on by itself.

## Episode
Title: Dead Air
Synopsis: Diana's first week on the job takes a terrifying turn when a caller claims to be someone who died thirty years ago. What starts as a prank becomes undeniable when the caller shares details no living person could know.
