# Sample StoryRPG Season Treatment

## 1. StoryRPG Structure Model

Season -> Acts -> Seven-Point Anchors + Buffers -> Episodes -> Episode Turns -> Scenes -> Beats

## 3. 3-Act / 7-Point Season Spine

- Hook: Mara accepts the lighthouse post.
- Plot Turn 1: The lantern answers with her sister's voice.
- Pinch 1: The town council threatens to burn the station.
- Midpoint: Mara learns the light is a prison, not a beacon.
- Pinch 2: Her ally sells the key to save his family.
- Climax: Mara opens the storm door during the king tide.
- Resolution: The harbor remembers what it buried.

## 7. Episode Outline

### Episode 1: The Lantern Job

- **Act:** Act 1
- **Structural role: anchor, fused anchors, or buffer:** Hook + Plot Turn 1
- **Episode promise:** Mara takes the lighthouse job and discovers the light can answer grief.
- **Episode turns:**
  - Mara arrives at the emptied lighthouse with her sister's scarf in her bag.
  - The keeper's ledger shows her name written years before she came.
  - The lantern speaks in her sister's voice when Mara lights it.
- **Tone register:** Salt-stung mystery with intimate dread.
- **Encounter anchor:** The first night watch traps Mara between the living harbor below and the impossible voice in the lantern.
- **How the encounter manifests the central conflict:** Mara must decide whether the voice is a miracle worth protecting or a trap wearing her grief.
- **Encounter buildup:** The town warns her not to light the old lens after midnight.
- **Aftermath / consequence:** The lantern leaves salt burns on Mara's hands and the harbor begins answering her footsteps.
- **Major choice pressure:** Tell the town what happened, or hide the voice to keep speaking with it.
- **Alternative paths:** Confession earns one cautious ally but puts the council on alert; secrecy gives Mara more time but isolates her.
- **Consequence seeds:** Salt burns, the keeper's ledger, and the sister's scarf.
- **Ending pressure:** At dawn, the lighthouse shadow points inland instead of out to sea.
- **Capability, Growth, And Fail-Forward:**
  - Failed investigation should expose a costlier clue rather than dead-ending the mystery.

### 2. Breakwater Oath

- **Act:** Act 2A
- **Structural role:** Pinch 1
- **Episode promise:** Mara tests whether anyone in town can be trusted.
- **Episode turns:** Mara follows the lighthouse shadow to the old breakwater; Jonas admits the council has hidden wreck records; the sea returns a drowned bell.
- **Encounter anchors:** The council hearing turns public suspicion into a social encounter with real reputation damage.
- **How the encounter manifests the central conflict:** Mara must protect the truth without proving the town right about her instability.
- **Aftermath / consequence:** Jonas loses his council post for helping her.
- **Major choice pressure:** Save Jonas's reputation or keep the ledger hidden.
- **Consequence seeds:** Jonas's broken oath and the drowned bell.
- **Ending pressure:** The bell rings underwater with Mara's sister counting down.

## 8. Cross-Episode Branches And Consequence Chains

### Branch A — The Ledger Confession

If Mara confesses in Episode 1, Episode 2 opens with guarded help. If she lies, the hearing begins hostile. The branch reconverges in Episode 2 after the drowned bell.

## 11. Episode Endings

### Ending 1 - "The Keeper"

- **Summary:** Mara becomes the new keeper and bargains with the harbor without freeing what sleeps below.
- **Emotional register:** Bittersweet vigilance.
- **Theme payoff:** Some grief becomes duty.
- **State drivers:** Relationship with Jonas; choice pattern around secrecy.
- **Target conditions:** Protect the lantern more often than exposing it.

### Ending 2 - "The Open Door"

- **Summary:** Mara frees the trapped dead and lets the lighthouse go dark.
- **Emotional register:** Grieving liberation.
- **Theme payoff:** Love releases what fear would keep.
- **State drivers:** Identity; encounter outcome at the storm door.
- **Target conditions:** Choose truth over safety.

### Ending 3 - "The Burned Harbor"

- **Summary:** The town destroys the light and the harbor takes its payment in names.
- **Emotional register:** Tragic reckoning.
- **Theme payoff:** Denial becomes inheritance.
- **State drivers:** Faction; failed alliance with the council.
- **Target conditions:** Lose public trust and fail to protect Jonas.
