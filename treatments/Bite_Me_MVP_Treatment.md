# Bite Me — Story Treatment (Season One)

*Converted to the Story Treatment MVP format. Source structure (3-act / 7-point spine) has been re-mapped onto the StoryRPG 8-beat Story Circle, with one primary beat per episode across an 8-episode season.*

---

## 1. Story Premise

- **Title:** Bite Me
- **Genre:** Paranormal rom-com that graduates into dark vampire romance. Champagne-and-cocktails comedy early, slow-burn supernatural romance by midseason, full horror-tinged dark fantasy by the finale. The in-world artifact is the blog *Dating After Dusk*.
- **Tone:** Champagne fizz on top, blood at the bottom. Witty, fashion-forward, friend-group banter; codename-protected blog posts; a savior in a charcoal suit; a chef who can't explain his monthly absences; a city that gets sexier and more dangerous the further in you go.
- **Logline:** A New York food writer fleeing a publicly-cancelled engagement arrives in Bucharest, starts a blog about her dating life under nicknames like Mr. Midnight and The Mountain, and slowly discovers that the man she's writing about, the friends she's writing it for, and the city she's writing from are all monsters of one kind or another — and that the only person she can fully trust is herself.
- **Core fantasy:** Be the new It girl in a beautiful foreign city — a friend group that orders one more round at dusk, a rescue by a man too good to be true, a slow-burn with a quieter man who is a different kind of trouble, slip dresses on cobblestones, a blog readership that climbs by the week — while the dawning recognition lands that the people who love you most might be lying about who they are.
- **Themes:** Reinvention after public heartbreak; adored vs. known (being chosen is not the same as being seen); whose voice is yours (authorship vs. being edited); what kind of monster you fall for before you learn what kind you refuse to become; female friendship surviving the worst possible confession.
- **Audience promise:** The first three episodes feel like vacation — slip dresses, codenamed bad dates, the most flatteringly-lit man you've ever seen across a velvet booth. Episodes 4–5 darken. By the back half you can't look away, as one friend at a time turns out to be something other than what Kylie thought — including the one she trusted most. The season delivers the intoxication of being adored after public humiliation, the slow horror of realizing adoration is annihilation in a tuxedo, and the triumph (or tragedy) of a heartbroken woman finally telling the story straight.

*Pressure that makes inaction impossible:* Victor stages his own meet-cute and begins courting Kylie the night she lands; the blog he cannot control drives readers to his door, so by midseason he asks her to give it up. Mika is delivering Kylie to him on a fifty-seven-year-old contract while genuinely falling in love with her. Stela has been quietly warding Kylie for weeks and can no longer wait to tell her she is hunted. Standing still means Kylie loses her humanity, her grandmother's legacy, her best friend, or her voice.

## 2. Season Anchors

- **Stakes:** Kylie's voice (the blog, growing from a few thousand readers to a possible 800K and carrying her own byline instead of her ex's); the Dusk Club friendships (Mika and Stela); her apartment as the one sanctuary Victor cannot enter; her humanity and mortality on the Hunter's Moon; her grandmother's near-extinct Marinescu line; and Mika's freedom from a fifty-seven-year contract.
- **Goal:** Build a life and a blog with her own name on it, decide which of two gorgeous men (if either) is worth keeping, and become the author of her story instead of its punchline — without letting any man or monster in the city edit her out of it.
- **Inciting incident:** On night three Kylie is attacked in Cișmigiu Gardens and rescued by a stranger in a charcoal suit. She writes about him as *Mr. Midnight*; the post does 80,000 reads in a week — and unknowingly fires the first shot in a courtship and a blog war she did not choose.
- **Climax:** On the Hunter's Moon at Casa Stelarum, in front of everyone she loves and an older entity that forces the matter, Kylie decides what she will and will not become for love: whose hand she takes, whether she stands at the salt circle to free Mika, whether she surrenders the blog, and whether claiming the Marinescu name and her own voice is brave or just lonely.

## 3. Story Circle Season Spine

One season-level beat per episode, eight episodes for eight beats.

- **You (Ep1):** Kylie's ordinary world is reinvention-as-performance. She arrives in Bucharest with two suitcases and her grandmother's address, gathers the Dusk Club over too-dark negronis, and protects herself the way she always has — by observing, ordering second, and writing the piece later. Opening promise: a heartbroken woman gets a glamorous new life and her own byline. The staged rescue and the viral *Mr. Midnight* post close the beat by making her a name.
- **Need (Ep2):** The contradiction surfaces. The viral blog makes Kylie visible enough that a centuries-old suitor and an anonymous tipster both want her attention; she walks toward Victor, finds the best conversation in five years, meets a second, rougher man on a mountain road, and ends the night holding two numbers and a warning — *Ileana is missing. Don't go.* What's missing: the truth about who is steering her, and her own permission to claim a story rather than only describe one.
- **Go (Ep3):** The threshold crossing. Kylie accepts the Equinox weekend at Casa Stelarum and, against her better judgment, lets herself be courted — the maze kiss is the moment her appetite outvotes her noticing. She crosses out of safe observation into the unfamiliar pressure field of being the woman in the story. A scarf on a doorstep she never gave anyone the address to closes the beat.
- **Search (Ep4):** Experiments, alliance, temptation, rising cost. The slow-burn weekend at Casa Lupului offers an honest, mortal alternative to Victor; Stela's consent-based warding upgrades; Victor makes his first explicit move on the blog; a DM says *ask The Mountain about 2015.* Kylie tests both lives and presses on what Radu won't say.
- **Find (Ep5):** The midpoint discovery that changes the meaning of the goal. Mid-undress, the mirror behind Victor shows Kylie standing alone; she runs to Stela, who opens the door with mugwort already burning and tells two truths — what Victor is, and that she herself is a hunter who has warded Kylie all along. The genre flips; the blog skips its first day; Veronica's 1961 photograph reveals the stag-crest ring.
- **Take (Ep6):** The real price. Kylie spends the last unspoiled relationship she came for — Radu confesses what he is — and the world stops having any exceptions. Carmen is beaten and Radu is framed; the blog war goes hot with *Don't Tell Me What to Write*; a black rose appears inside her warded apartment, proof Victor can reach her through the people she loves.
- **Return (Ep7):** The reckoning that brings the answer home. Kylie confronts the friend she trusts most; Mika confesses fifty-seven years and asks to be told what to do. Stela offers to dissolve the contract on the Hunter's Moon. Kylie arms the finale — the loaded drafts post, the clan question, who to bring to the moon — and the courtyard dog stands up as Radu.
- **Change (Ep8):** The changed self and world. On the Hunter's Moon at Casa Stelarum every thread pulls tight; an older Strigoi Mama's emissary forces the matter of the Marinescu line. Kylie authors one of three irreversible lives — the Consort, the Mountain Wife, or the Witness — and writes the season's final post in her own voice. *(The final confrontation/return completes inside this beat; with eight episodes the climax and resolution fuse here.)*

## 4. Protagonist Brief

- **Name and pronouns:** Kylie Marinescu (she/her).
- **Role in the world:** 34, American food writer turned blogger; quarter-Romanian on her father's side. Her grandmother Veronica escaped Bucharest in 1962 and never explained why. Six months ago Kylie's engagement to a New York restaurateur, Daniel Hayes, was publicly called off after she found he'd been sleeping with his sommelier for a year; half her industry knew, two food magazines ran items. She took the venue deposit back, packed two suitcases, and used Veronica's old address as a landing pad.
- **Want:** Build a blog with her own name on it instead of her ex's, have a sex life and friends again, and figure out which of two gorgeous men is worth keeping — to be the writer of the story this time, not the punchline.
- **Need:** To author her own life on her own terms — to claim her own appetite and her own voice, and to learn that being safely adored is not the same as being known or being free.
- **Lie:** Being chosen by a beautiful man is the same as being known and safe; her job is to observe and describe other people's lives, apologizing for any feeling of her own, because a woman's voice is something men edit or take.
- **Wound or origin pressure:** She gave a man five years and he apparently did not know her, or did not want to; the cancelled engagement broke her sense of being known, publicly. She believes she's brave because she got on a plane; she is, in fact, still flinching. Underneath sits her grandmother's unexplained flight from Romania — a family pattern of women fleeing men who wanted to own them.
- **Truth:** Her voice belongs to her. Being adored is not the same as being seen, and the bravest thing a heartbroken woman can do is fall all the way for someone who tells her the truth — or refuse all of them and tell her own story straight.
- **Arc mode:** Positive (transformation) — from observer who orders second and writes the piece later, to author who claims the Marinescu name and her own column. *(The Consort ending is the tragic inversion, in which the Lie wins.)*
- **Climax choice:** On the Hunter's Moon, before everyone she loves and an older entity forcing the matter, Kylie decides whose hand she takes, whether she stands at the salt circle for Mika, whether she gives Victor the blog, and whether the third path — keep your friends, your blog, and yourself — is brave or just lonely.
- **Starting identity:** The observer. The one who orders second, watches the room, and writes the piece later. Charming, perceptive, conflict-avoidant. Drinks champagne to seem like she's having a feeling. A brilliant noticer who second-guesses herself so long she misses windows.
- **Possible end states:**
  - **The Consort** — accepts permanence with Victor; the blog goes dark; her byline disappears (tragic-glamorous).
  - **The Mountain Wife** — chooses Radu and a mortal, honest life in the Apuseni; the blog becomes a quiet bestselling book; she stays mortal (bittersweet-warm).
  - **The Witness** — chooses herself; Mika is freed and forgiven, Stela stays, the Dusk Club survives, *Dating After Dusk* triples (triumphant-warm).
  - **The Hollowed** (failure-adjacent, not a fourth target) — tries to keep both men and please everyone, loses Stela's trust and Mika to Victor, gives up the blog without committing, and ends smaller than she started.
- **Pressure points:** Her grandmother's flight from Romania and the near-extinct Marinescu line; her niece Sadie (7) in Boston, whose photo sits on her desk; the unfinished memoir abandoned at chapter two; the fact that being chosen makes her stupid and being safe makes her bored.
- **Visual identity:** Mid-30s, honey-blonde hair she trims herself, tortoiseshell glasses worn only to write, slip dresses under a thrown-on trench, her grandmother's gold chain at her throat, a perpetually half-charged phone, lipstick blotted off after the first sip.

*Strength that causes problems / weakness that helps: her gift as a noticer made the blog and catches every tell — and forces her to ask the questions that destroy the relationships her Lie said she could keep safe by observing instead of claiming.*

## 5. Major NPC Briefs

### Victor Vâlcescu ("Mr. Midnight")

- **Role:** Antagonist (disguised as love interest). Strigoi (vampire); mysterious savior in Ep1 → confident suitor → midpoint reveal → seductive dark-romance lead → season antagonist by Ep6. Owns Vâlcescu Club on Calea Victoriei and the country estate Casa Stelarum.
- **Want:** To possess Kylie forever — narcissistically, not romantically — and to make the blog stop. He wants to turn her, marry her in a coven ceremony at Casa Stelarum, end *Dating After Dusk*, and keep her in the night as his dark bride.
- **Leverage:** A boyar fortune and hospitality empire, immortality, a country estate, the social calendar of every powerful person in the country, the ability to make Kylie feel like the only woman in any room, and media holdings (residency, book deal, private print column) he uses to make and unmake careers — and to pressure the blog.
- **Secret or contradiction:** He cannot tell the difference between loving Kylie and owning her; his idea of love is an exclusive contract, and he is genuinely not lying about loving her. Kylie's grandmother Veronica was his fiancée in 1961 and escaped the night before her turning; he has been sixty years politely furious and is unsure whether he loves Kylie or is settling an inheritance. The Ep1 "attacker" was a low-tier coven enforcer on his payroll, told to scare not bite — he staged his own meet-cute, with Mika ensuring Kylie walked through that park at that hour.
- **Relationship to protagonist:** Suitor and savior who curdles into possessor; the man she could fall for, who is not lying about loving her and not telling the whole truth either.
- **How player choices can change the relationship:** Walking over to him at the rooftop, drinking the dark wine, agreeing to wind the blog down, and going home with him before the mirror tilt toward the Consort. Refusing the wine, holding the blog, and refusing his last ballroom offer turn his charm to leverage and lock him out.
- **Voice notes:** Low, even, too-perfect light accent. Calls her *darling girl* once early and never after she winces. Loves a long pause. Asks questions whose answers he already knows. Compliments her writing in a way that makes her wonder how long he's been reading.
- **Visual identity:** 6'1", lean, dark hair silvering at the temples without progressing, Milan-cut charcoal three-piece suits, a stag-crested signet ring on his right hand, a faint unexplained jaw scar, never seen eating, always drinking blood-dark wine in a Zalto glass.

### Radu Stoian ("The Mountain")

- **Role:** Love interest (second lead). Pricolici (werewolf) of the Stoian pack near Bran; roadside meet-cute → sweet flirtation → midseason heartbreak → real partner if Kylie chooses him. Runs Casa Lupului, a working mountain lodge and restaurant.
- **Want:** To live a decent life and not become his father; to love Kylie without lying — which means telling her, which means losing her, so he doesn't tell her until he has to. He quietly hates being on the blog even as *The Mountain* but respects her work too much to ask her to stop.
- **Leverage:** A lodge and restaurant, a pack-family of cousins who would die for him, the knowledge of how to keep someone alive in a Carpathian winter, a body that runs warm in a country that is always cold.
- **Secret or contradiction:** Once a month he is not a man. He was at the rooftop bar the night Kylie landed, was struck by her, had a bad feeling, and tried to follow her home through the park fog — but Victor's people got there first. He won't tell her until Episode 6/7. His last girlfriend (2015) asked him to never do something again. He equates honesty with abandonment, so he hides the curse behind what reads to Kylie as old grief.
- **Relationship to protagonist:** The honest second lead; the quiet life she could choose; the man whose confession costs her the third thing she came to Bucharest looking for.
- **How player choices can change the relationship:** Holding his look at the kitchen entrance in Ep1 makes him confess earlier. Staying the night at Casa Lupului and sitting an extra five minutes after his Ep6 confession opens the Mountain Wife ending fully; walking out cold leaves it cold.
- **Voice notes:** Slow, careful English with mountain Romanian underneath. Calls her *Kylie-girl* once and is embarrassed. Short sentences, long silences. Apologizes by cooking.
- **Visual identity:** 38, 6'2", broad, short dark beard, three parallel scars inside his left forearm, hand-knit sweaters his late grandmother made, mud on his boots, woodsmoke and bay leaf in his hair, amber eyes that go gold under moonlight.

### Mika Drăgan ("The Samantha")

- **Role:** Ally turned betrayer turned reconcilable friend. Best friend; head of door and talent at Vâlcescu Club; owner of Drăgan Vintage on Lipscani; the loudest laugh at the table. Final-three-episodes reveal: a succubus contracted to Victor's coven since 1968, placed in Kylie's life three months before Kylie arrived.
- **Want:** Two things in fifty-seven-year conflict — to do what Victor tells her (which has kept her free and fed since the contract) and to be loved as herself by a girl who actually likes her, which she hasn't been in decades.
- **Leverage:** Knows every door in Bucharest; has dressed three generations of First Ladies; has time, money, taste, and no shame; is bound to Victor by a magical contract Stela could break if Stela knew about it.
- **Secret or contradiction:** Made/contracted by Victor in 1968; "running into" Kylie since the sublet was signed; set up the rooftop bar (Ep1) and the cab breakdown (Ep2) — but not Radu, a wildcard. She frames her body count as moral math (she only feeds on bad men), a self-justification Stela later challenges. She genuinely, painfully loves Kylie while genuinely delivering her.
- **Relationship to protagonist:** The friend Kylie trusts most; the betrayer whose betrayal is reconcilable; the confession at the warm center of the back half.
- **How player choices can change the relationship:** Catch her tells gently and early (the missing hour, never-at-brunch, the diet, the too-dark negroni, always at the club) and her loyalty shifts toward Kylie episodes before the reveal; catch her cruelly and she returns to Victor. Forgive her at the climax and Stela frees her at the salt circle; refuse and she walks into the morning with him.
- **Voice notes:** Crackling, low-laughing; code-switches Romanian/French/English mid-sentence. Calls everyone *iubita mea* and means it about half the time. Quotes movies that came out before Kylie was born. Tells: *brunch is a crime against women*, *I'm on a diet*, the house negroni one shade too dark.
- **Visual identity:** Looks 32. Platinum bob, red lacquered lips, leopard under emerald silk, vintage Romanian gold at the throat, a small puncture scar inside her left wrist hidden by bracelets, sunglasses indoors after 11am, never eats at the table.

### Stela Pavel (the Earth Mother with a Family Secret)

- **Role:** Ally and truth-teller; the offered third path. Hippie-chick bookshop friend and sex-positive truth-teller; runs Lumina Books on Lipscani. Midseason (Ep5) reveal: a Romani folk practitioner from a clan of strigoi hunters in the Apuseni, the daughter who walked away but never stopped practicing.
- **Want:** To keep the women Victor circles alive, to atone, and to live a modern, queer, sex-positive life on her own terms without betraying the old ways.
- **Leverage:** Folk-magic warding, blood-warnings, herb-binding, candle and tarot divination, sex magic; a bookshop that doubles as a meeting place for women who know things; the knowledge of how to kill a strigoi and break a coven contract; three sisters and a brother in the Apuseni who can reach Bucharest in six hours.
- **Secret or contradiction:** Her belief that you do not tell a woman she is hunted until she is ready to hear it — a protective discipline that withholds the truth from Kylie for weeks. Her partner before her Amsterdam girlfriend, Jonas, was turned in 2014; she killed him herself with a silver pin and a salt circle and has done penance since by warding the women she sees Victor circle. She has been warding Kylie's apartment since the housewarming through the quartz, the herb bag, and the candle.
- **Relationship to protagonist:** The most reliable source of truth; the friend who opens the door at 2am with mugwort already burning; the offered third path.
- **How player choices can change the relationship:** Curiosity about the bookshop, the moon phases, and her offerings earns trust; sitting through an herb-burning or taking up the sex-magic conversation earns love. Dismissing her as "the woo-woo one" cuts off Kylie's truest source and gets people hurt. Confiding in her by Ep4 begins her investigation early and is required for the Witness ending.
- **Voice notes:** Warm, low, deliberate. English with a Romanian undertow and Amsterdam vowels. Calls Kylie *love* and means it. Pauses to feel her feelings before answering. Knows the moon phase without checking. Calls her family *my people* and her clan *my line.*
- **Visual identity:** 39, sun-warm olive skin, long dark hair in a loose plait, gold hoops she never removes, a wide gold bangle from her grandmother, layered linen and embroidered shawls from her sister's loom, ink and herb stains on her fingers, no makeup, a quartz pendant she touches when thinking.

### Carmen Iliescu (the no-profile-photo account)

- **Role:** Wildcard ally. Investigative journalist who has tracked the Vâlcescu coven's victims for two years; the anonymous DM warner who becomes a named ally in the back half.
- **Want:** To expose the coven and publish the names of its victims; to stop Victor before more women disappear.
- **Leverage:** A two-year spreadsheet of the coven's victims; a byline at *Adevărul*; the credibility to convulse the city's supernatural society when she publishes.
- **Secret or contradiction:** Her identity behind the no-profile-photo account; the depth of her file; her belief that she can warn and investigate from anonymity without becoming a target — which breaks when she is attacked.
- **Relationship to protagonist:** Anonymous guardian angel turned co-conspirator; the outside proof that Kylie isn't imagining the danger.
- **How player choices can change the relationship:** Agreeing to meet immediately, asking Stela to come along, or stalling changes how exposed Carmen and Kylie are; asking her to publish the spreadsheet first vs. begging her to wait shifts how hot the blog war and the finale run.
- **Voice notes:** Clipped, factual, urgent — the cadence of someone careful for two years; warmer once unmasked.
- **Visual identity:** Plain, deliberately unmemorable in person; a reporter's bag; later bruised and bandaged in a hospital bed, still talking to police.

## 6. World And Location Brief

- **World premise:** Modern Bucharest, present day. On the surface, a beautiful contradictory city of Belle Époque facades and Communist blocks, third-wave coffee and grandmother soups, a club scene punching above its weight. Underneath, revealed only in stages, a thriving supernatural society older than the country's current name: strigoi run the nightlife and the real estate, pricolici keep to the Carpathians, Romani practitioners keep score and keep women alive, and contracted succubi feed glamorously through the dating scene.
- **Time period:** Now. Phones, blogs, dating apps, content creators, brand deals, Aperol, post-pandemic real estate prices.
- **Technology/magic/supernatural rules:** Kept simple and almost entirely off-page until Episode 5.
  - **Strigoi** (vampires, Victor's kind) are made, not born; require invitation to enter dwellings unless "broken"; cannot cross fresh-flowing water unassisted; show in mirrors but not in screens (phone cameras miss them); direct sunlight weakens but does not destroy; cannot lie about their nature when asked directly, but can refuse to answer.
  - **Pricolici** (werewolves, Radu's kind) are inherited through the male line; transform involuntarily monthly until the early thirties, then partly by will; silver burns but does not kill; a full-moon bite on a lover turns, kills, or breaks them — and no one knows which until it is over.
  - **Succubi** (and one incubus) are contracted, not born; feed on sexual life force; cannot operate before dusk; bound to a coven by a magical contract a practitioner of rank can dissolve before sunrise, with both parties' consent, inside a salt circle.
  - **Romani folk practitioners** are trained in warding, scrying, blood-warning, herb-binding, sex magic, and — in the hunter clans — the killing of strigoi; they cannot fight a strigoi bare-handed but can make a room hostile to one and can break a coven contract.
- **Power structures:** The Vâlcescu Coven (Victor's strigoi, fronting as a hospitality empire; the club is the lure); the Stoian Pack (Radu's pricolici, fronting as restaurateurs, shepherds, and rangers); the Pavel Clan (Stela's traditional hunting line, two valleys over); the Coven's Bound (three succubi and one incubus, Mika senior among them); the near-extinct Marinescu line (Veronica's unfinished business); and, above all of them, an older Strigoi Mama who has watched this coven for two centuries.
- **Rules that create drama:**
  - You cannot un-invite a strigoi once they cross your threshold; the only fix is to break the dwelling and move.
  - A practitioner cannot ward a friend without that friend's symbolic consent — Stela's wards depend on Kylie having accepted the quartz, the herb bag, and the candle.
  - A pricolici who bites a lover on a full moon turns, kills, or breaks them, with no way to know which.
  - A succubus contract can be dissolved only by a practitioner of rank, with both parties consenting, inside a salt circle, before sunrise.
  - Strigoi cannot be photographed — which makes a public, codenamed blog about one a weapon over which he has no control.

### Key Locations

- **Vâlcescu Club (Calea Victoriei)** — Victor's club. Velvet booths the color of dried blood, candlelight only, no clocks or windows, a Thursday jazz quartet, a back room nobody talks about; Mika runs the door. *Purpose:* the funnel. *Mood:* seductive, timeless, airless. *History:* a hospitality empire over a feeding ground. *Choice pressure:* how long you stay, whether you go to the back room, whether you let him walk you home.
- **Cișmigiu Gardens** — Bucharest's central park: willows, lake, fog. *Purpose:* the Ep1 attack-and-rescue and the climactic dawn walk. *Mood:* glamour turning to dread. *History:* the site of Victor's staged meet-cute. *Choice pressure:* scream, run, freeze, or fight in Ep1; walk past, stop, or say goodbye to Victor at dawn.
- **Casa Lupului (near Bran)** — Radu's mountain lodge and restaurant. Wood, woodsmoke, found antlers, a fire-run kitchen, a guest room with a key only Kylie is given. *Purpose:* the honest, mortal alternative. *Mood:* warm, plain, true. *History:* pack-family home. *Choice pressure:* stay the night or drive back; sit five more minutes after the confession or walk out cold.
- **Casa Stelarum** — Victor's restored boyar estate an hour north. Gold-leaf ballrooms, hunting grounds, a cellar of very old wine and very old other things; site of the Equinox Ball and the Hunter's Moon finale. *Mood:* golden seduction over the grave. *History:* the coven's seat; Veronica's letter is hidden in a wall safe here. *Choice pressure:* drink the dark wine, kiss in the maze, the finale's three offers and one speech.
- **Lumina Books (Lipscani)** — Stela's bookshop. Romanian poetry and folklore, herbs in jars, crystals in the window, a tarot table, a back room with a salt circle painted under the rug; patchouli, rosemary, and good coffee. *Purpose:* the truth-place; sanctuary at 2am. *Mood:* warm, woven, safe. *History:* fifteen years of Stela warding the women Victor circles. *Choice pressure:* accept the quartz/herbs/candle, hear all of Stela's reveal or not.
- **Kylie's apartment (Lipscani)** — Belle Époque fourth-floor walk-up, balcony over a courtyard, secretly warded by Stela since the housewarming — the only place Victor cannot enter, if the wards hold. *Purpose:* the consent-and-sanctuary test. *Mood:* hers, fragile, lamplit. *Choice pressure:* every invitation question and every ward upgrade.

### Episode 1: Dating After Dusk

- **Act:** Act 1
- **Story Circle role:** `you` (primary beat).
- **Role note:** This episode owns `you` because it establishes Kylie's ordinary world and protection strategy (observe, order second, write it later) and the opening promise (reinvention with her own byline). It cannot move later because every downstream reveal — the staged rescue, Mika's placement, the quartz consent, Radu's first sighting — is planted here; nothing the season pays off exists until this beat lands.
- **Episode promise:** Can a woman fleeing a publicly-cancelled engagement actually start over — make friends, feel wanted, and write under her own name — in a city that doesn't know her ex's name?
- **Episode turns:**
  1. Mika adopts Kylie at the door of Vâlcescu Club on night two, swaps out her "American shoes," and hands her a key card to the side entrance.
  2. At a Lipscani bookshop, Stela presses a chunk of rose quartz into Kylie's hand — *this one wants to be with you, love* — and the Dusk Club is three.
  3. On night three at a rooftop bar at sunset, the friends vow never to date a man with a podcast; Kylie locks eyes with Victor across the room while Mika murmurs *let's eat first* and goes still for half a second.
  4. Walking home through Cișmigiu at 1am, Kylie is pinned to a willow by a shadow — and a second figure in a charcoal suit drops the attacker, walks her home, kisses her hand at the threshold, declines to come in, and vanishes.
  5. At 4am, unable to sleep, Kylie launches *Dating After Dusk* with a post about a club, a friend who calls everyone *iubita*, and the man she names only Mr. Midnight; by 6pm it has 80,000 reads.
- **Synopsis:** Kylie arrives in Bucharest with two suitcases and her grandmother's address, collecting a friend at the door of a velvet-booth club and another in a folklore bookshop, then christening the Dusk Club over too-dark negronis. On her foggy walk home through Cișmigiu she is attacked and rescued by the impossibly handsome stranger she'd been watching from across the rooftop bar, who walks her home and disappears. ## 9. Cross-Episode Branches And Consequence Chains

### Branch A — The Quartz (Sanctuary vs. Open Threshold)
- **Created by:** At Lumina Books (Ep1), Kylie accepts the rose quartz (canonical), declines it, or buys it for cash and tosses it in her bag unlooked-at. Accepting it gives Stela's wards their required symbolic consent.
- **Changes later:** With the quartz accepted and the Ep4 candle ritual completed, the apartment holds at Ep6 — Victor cannot cross, and the black rose has to come through Mika's hand. With it refused or lost, the wards never take, and Victor walks in uninvited in Ep6, collapsing her only sanctuary.
- **Reconvergence:** Ep5 (the mirror lands either way); the access consequence cashes out in Ep6.
- **Residue:** Whether the apartment is full sanctuary, partial, or compromised — carried into the finale and Season 2. The quartz must be accepted and kept for both the Mountain Wife and Witness endings.

### Branch B — The Blog War (Hard-Line Voice vs. Conciliation)
- **Created by:** A pattern of blog decisions under Victor's escalating pressure — the Ep3 pre-weekend post, the Ep5 "consider winding the blog down" ask, publishing *Don't Tell Me What to Write* in Ep6, and publishing or shelving the confessional post in Ep7.
- **Changes later:** Hard-line drives readership toward 800K and forces Victor from charm to leverage (media holdings, a framed journalist, a final ultimatum); conciliation softens his pressure through Ep6 but locks in his expectation that the blog ends, tipping the finale toward his terms. Publishing the Ep7 drafts post takes the blog from ~470K to ~800K overnight.
- **Reconvergence:** Ep8 (the ballroom) — what Victor asks for, one last time, depends entirely on what Kylie has been writing.
- **Residue:** The final readership number, the codename dictionary quoted verbatim in the finale post, and whether her voice was defended or surrendered. The blog must not have been wound down for the Witness ending; winding it down feeds the Consort.

### Branch C — The Country-House Wine (A New Appetite vs. None)
- **Created by:** At the Equinox toast (Ep3), Kylie drinks the dark unlabeled wine, sips and sets it down, or refuses it.
- **Changes later:** Drinking it fully seeds a craving that surfaces as a hand-tremor at the Ep5 mirror and Mika's Ep7 confession — a body-level pull toward Victor's world at the moments she's trying to pull away. Refusing leaves no craving but is noticed at the table.
- **Reconvergence:** Ep7 (the tremor's last appearance), feeding the Ep8 climax.
- **Residue:** Whether Kylie carries a body-level draw toward Victor into the ballroom. Drinking fully is a Consort driver; refusing supports the Mountain Wife and Witness.

### Branch D — Mika's Crossroad (Read Gently vs. Read Cruelly)
- **Created by:** A pattern across Eps 1, 3, 4, 6, 7 of whether Kylie notices and how she handles Mika's tells — the half-second of stillness, the missing hour, the conveniently-broken cab, *never at brunch* and *on a diet*, the too-dark negroni, always at the club.

At the Ep7 crossroad, *I want to help free you* vs. *I can't see you again* decides whether she kneels at the salt circle or leaves on Victor's arm.
- **Reconvergence:** Ep8 (the salt circle at moonrise).
- **Residue:** Whether Mika is freed from her fifty-seven-year contract or walks out with Victor — and whether the Dusk Club survives. The gentle read and the choice to free her are prerequisites for the Witness ending.

### Branch E — The Mountain Confession (Stay and Sit vs. Leave Cold)
- **Created by:** Whether Kylie stays the night at Casa Lupului (Ep4) and then sits an extra five minutes after Radu's full confession (Ep6) or walks out cold.
- **Changes later:** Staying and sitting makes the Ep6 confession land warm and keeps a real partnership open — by Ep7 Radu is on her balcony and at her side toward the finale; leaving after dinner in Ep4 makes the confession land as a stranger's secret, and walking out cold closes the partnership.
- **Reconvergence:** Ep6 (the kitchen confession), with the partnership's viability carried to Ep8.
- **Residue:** Whether Radu is a man Kylie could choose a life with or a closed door — and whether he drives down to the ball or waits, reachable, in the foothills. Staying and sitting are required to open the Mountain Wife ending fully.

### Smaller consequence chains
- **The wine craving:** Branch C residue surfaces as a hand-tremor in Eps 5 and 7, a recurring physical tell of Victor's hold.
- **The hand-knit scarf:** Radu's Ep3 doormat gift threads through his arc and appears in the season's final shot.
- **The blog readership number:** Referenced at every episode's end (3K → 80K → 130K → 180K → 240K → 310K → 380K → 470K → variable), a running scoreboard of Kylie's reclaimed voice; becomes the most-read English-language blog in Romania only in the Witness ending.

- **Carmen's spreadsheet:** Seeded as the no-profile DMs from Ep2, referenced in the published *Adevărul* piece in Ep8.
- **The courtyard dog:** The stray that watches Kylie's window from Ep1 is revealed in Ep7 to be Radu.

## 10. Capability, Growth, And Fail-Forward

- **Key challenges:** The season tests perception, intimacy, voice, and nerve — never physical skill. The park attack (scream, run, freeze, fight); reading a room full of beautiful liars at Vâlcescu Club and Casa Stelarum (Mika's half-second of stillness, the photograph without Victor, the maiden name spoken at the wrong table); the courtship gauntlet (the maze kiss, the unlabeled wine, how much to let herself be wanted); the slow-burn at Casa Lupului (drawing out Radu's hidden grief without spooking him); the mirror moment (confront, run, or pretend); the friendship reckoning at Drăgan Vintage; the blog war (every soften/codename/residency/take-it-down request); the investigation thread (Carmen's DMs and spreadsheet, the Ileana mystery); and the Hunter's Moon ballroom (holding her own voice before three factions, an emissary, and an entity older than all of them). Each tests her relationships, her identity (observer vs. author), her access (the wards, the club rope, the Pavel clan, Radu's pack), and her prior choices.
- **How failure opens new routes (fail-forward, not game over):** A misread converts into a harder, richer route. Refusing the quartz leaves the apartment a compromised sanctuary, so Victor walks the threshold uninvited in Ep6 and Kylie must recover ground through Stela's later wardings and a move-or-break decision into Season 2. Walking over to Victor at the rooftop opens a small Mika lie she can catch later — failure as investigation fuel. Drinking the dark wine seeds a craving that becomes a clue and a pressure, not a loss screen. Reading Mika cruelly closes the cleanest Witness path but opens the harder, lonelier ballroom. Missing Radu's almost-confession by leaving early is recoverable by sitting an extra five minutes in the kitchen. The Carmen attack and the framing turn a failed meeting into a new alliance, a published exposé, and alternate leverage. Even the degraded Hollowed outcome is not a dead end but the undershot form of a real ending — the season always lands Kylie somewhere she can keep writing from. Failure consistently opens preparation (escalating wards, the salt circle, the clan), recovery (the bookshop floor at 3am, the balcony at dawn), alliance (Carmen, the cousins, the clan), investigation (the DMs, re-reading Mika's texts), alternate leverage (the blog's readership, the spreadsheet), or a harder route (the lonelier ballroom, the compromised apartment).
- **How growth stays fiction-first:** Kylie does not level up; she changes, visibly, in the fiction. Her progression is rendered as voice (drafts moving from breathless to defiant — the leap to *Don't Tell Me What to Write* and the unpublished nuclear post), trust (Stela opening up only once Kylie has earned it through the quartz, herb-bag, candle; Mika's loyalty quietly shifting when caught kindly), access (the side-entrance key card, the velvet rope, the guest-room key only Kylie is given, the Pavel clan that comes if called), reputation (the blog growing from 3,000 to a possible 800K, brand deals, a hashtag, becoming the most-read English blog in the country), ward strength (sanctuary, partial, or breached — felt as whether Victor can cross her door, never as a number), and appetite (the wine-craving and her widening permission to want). Mastery shows up as nerve and clarity in scenes — a steadier hand at the laptop, a question asked point-blank, a name said out loud in a ballroom — never as stats, levels, percentages, thresholds, or grind.

## 11. Episode Endings

- **E1 (`you`) — Dating After Dusk:** Closes "can a woman fleeing a cancelled engagement start over here?" (yes — two friends, a key card, an 80K blog overnight). Opens what the glamorous city is actually made of and who saved her. Forward turn: **Why did Stela wake from a nightmare convinced Kylie was in danger, and what does she know that Kylie doesn't?** Reveal/danger: Stela's herbs-and-warning arrival becomes Ep2's central pressure. Not fake withholding — the SatC start lands and the rescue is fully real; only the supernatural floor is held, with Stela's nightmare giving honest runway.
- **E2 (`need`) — Mr. Midnight:** Closes "is Mr. Midnight worth the risk, and can she find a second man worth writing about?" (yes and yes). Opens how far she'll look at what she's writing about, at what cost to her safety. Forward turn: **how brave is she willing to be about who Mr. Midnight really is — and who is the no-photo account warning her about Ileana?** Decision: the weekend invitation forces a yes/no on Victor at the moment a faceless source says don't go. Not fake withholding — three real dates and one real meet-cute; the Ileana warning is concrete.
- **E3 (`go`) — The Weekend:** Closes "what does Kylie look like when she lets herself be courted?" (golden — the maze kiss, the lucky feeling, a 50K post). Opens which man is honest and what the other is after. Forward turn: **which of these two men is courting her honestly — and what is the other one actually after?** Mystery/reveal: the scarf at an address she never gave, the courtyard dog watching. Not fake withholding — the courtship pays off as a real high; the two-suitors question is sharpened by hard clues (the missing reflection, the address).
- **E4 (`search`) — The Mountain:** Closes "is there a version where Kylie chooses the quiet life?" (yes — tempting and real). Opens whether Radu's refuge is safe or a curse she can't read. Forward turn: **is the quiet life he offers a refuge from Victor, or just a different kind of curse?** Reveal/relationship: the *ask about 2015* DM and Victor's first explicit blog demand. Not fake withholding — the quiet-life fantasy is playable; Radu's curse is foreshadowed with concrete tells.
- **E5 (`find`) — Mirror, Mirror:** Closes "what does Kylie want when she lets herself want?" (she wants him — then the mirror takes it away). Opens who else isn't what they seem and what Victor does about being seen. Forward turn: **now that Kylie knows what Victor is, who is still just a person, and what will Victor do now that she's seen?** Recontextualization: the quartz/herb/candle reframed as warding; Victor's *we should talk* closes distance. Not fake withholding — the genre flip is paid in full; the ring photo is a real new clue.
- **E6 (`take`) — Wolf at the Door:** Closes "is there anyone who is just a person?" (the answer narrows cruelly). Opens whether Victor can reach Kylie through her warm, weeping best friend. Forward turn: **is there anyone left — even her best friend — who is just a person and not his instrument?** Relationship rupture/cost: the black rose names Mika as the breach. Not fake withholding — Radu's confession is a complete turn; the rose is a concrete betrayal pointing at the next reveal.
- **E7 (`return`) — Best Friend:** Closes "can Kylie hold on to the friend she loves most?" (Mika confesses everything and asks what to do; the friendship survives, changed). Opens who Kylie brings to the Hunter's Moon and what she'll become for the people she loves. Forward turn: **who does Kylie bring to the Hunter's Moon — and what will she and won't she become for the people she loves?** Choice consequence/arrival: Radu's return reopens the romance. Not fake withholding — the friendship is genuinely reckoned with; the finale question is the natural sum of the player's choices.
- **E8 (`change`) — Bite Me (resolution/aftermath):**
  - **What resolves:** Who Kylie Marinescu is — answered in action on the Hunter's Moon, with the emissary forcing the matter and the Marinescu line settled. The blog war, Mika's contract, and the two-suitors question all land here.
  - **What was saved or lost:** Depends on the ending. **Consort** saves the relationship and loses her voice (blog dark, byline gone, Mika unfreed, Stela shut out). **Mountain Wife** saves her honesty and mortality and loses the city (she leaves Bucharest, the blog becomes a quiet bestselling book, Mika freed, Victor's white rose accepted after a pause). **Witness** saves friends, blog, and self (Mika's contract dissolved, Veronica's line reclaimed aloud, the apartment held, the Dusk Club survives). In every ending Veronica's letter surfaces and is mailed regardless.
  - **What the protagonist becomes:** A dark bride owned in gilded silence (Consort), a mortal wife in honest, difficult exile (Mountain Wife), or the author of her own life (Witness). The Hollowed is not a fourth fate but the undershot version of whichever life she reached for and missed.
  - **Future/legacy:** Veronica's reclaimed line and letter, Sadie's coming visit, Carmen's new column, Stela's apprenticeship and clan now entangled with Kylie's, and — on the Witness path — *Dating After Dusk* as the most-read English-language blog in the country, with Mika reopening Drăgan Vintage in daylight: proof a heartbroken woman can stop being the punchline and become the writer of her own story. The Strigoi Mama withdraws only "for now," carrying her threat into Season 2.

## 12. Alternate Endings

Exactly three season ending targets. The Hollowed is not a fourth ending — it is the degraded, undershot form of whichever of the three Kylie was reaching for and failed to fully earn (keeping both men, losing Stela's trust, losing Mika to Victor, giving up the blog without committing — the Lie reasserted by hedging).

### Ending 1: The Consort ("The Last Sunrise")
- **Summary:** Kylie accepts Victor's offer at Casa Stelarum on the Hunter's Moon, agreeing to wind down *Dating After Dusk* in exchange for the magazine residency he arranged; the final post is a goodbye titled *Off the Record.* The turning takes place in the rose garden at moonrise: Stela is blocked at the gate, Mika is on Victor's arm and never freed (Kylie did not stand at the salt circle). The emissary withdraws her claim on the Marinescu line — for now. Sadie visits in spring and Kylie pretends she has a sun allergy. Veronica's letter goes into a drawer unopened. Final voiceover: *Some women are loved. Some women are owned. I used to think those were the same thing.*
- **Emotional register:** Tragic-glamorous — a bittersweet ascension that reads as triumph and lands as annihilation in a tuxedo.
- **Theme payoff:** The Lie wins, beautifully. Being chosen by a beautiful man is mistaken, one last time, for being known and safe; Kylie hands her voice to the man who says he loves her and the byline disappears. Adoration consumes the self that authored it.
- **State drivers:** Victor-aligned choices across the season — agreeing to consider winding the blog down at the Ep5 lunch, drinking the dark wine fully (Branch C), going home with Victor in Ep5 before the mirror, the conciliatory side of the blog war (Branch B), reading Mika cruelly or not at all (Branch D), the quartz refused or lost (Branch A).
- **Target conditions:** Victor-aligned pattern across at least five of the eight episode-level major choices, including the Hunter's Moon wine and the blog wind-down agreement. The quartz must be refused or lost. Mika does not need to survive free.

### Ending 2: The Mountain Wife ("Howl Through the Carpathians")
- **Summary:** Kylie chooses Radu. She helps Carmen publish the spreadsheet, walks into Casa Stelarum on Radu's arm at moonrise, makes a short speech, refuses Victor on his own ballroom floor, and leaves the city at dawn. By spring she's at Casa Lupului full-time; *Dating After Dusk* becomes a book — *Mr. Midnight and Other Men* — that becomes a quiet international bestseller. She stays mortal. She marries Radu on a Tuesday in May, with Stela, a newly-free Mika, and Sadie present. Victor sends a single white rose in a clean glass box, and Kylie, after a long pause, accepts it. Final voiceover: *It turns out the bravest thing a heartbroken woman can do is fall, again, all the way, for somebody who tells you the truth.*
- **Emotional register:** Bittersweet-warm — hard-won peace bought with the city she rebuilt herself in.
- **Theme payoff:** The Truth in its romantic register — the bravest thing a heartbroken woman can do is fall all the way for someone who tells her the truth. She chooses the honest, difficult, mortal life over the flattering lie, and keeps her voice by turning the blog into a book that carries her name, not her ex's.
- **State drivers:** Radu-aligned choices — staying the night at Casa Lupului in Ep4 and sitting the extra five minutes after the Ep6 confession (Branch E), publishing *Don't Tell Me What to Write* (Branch B), not drinking the dark wine (Branch C), the quartz accepted and kept (Branch A).
- **Target conditions:** Radu-aligned pattern across at least five of the eight episode-level major choices. The quartz must be accepted and kept.

### Ending 3: The Witness ("Dating After Dusk")
- **Summary:** Kylie chooses neither man. She walks into Casa Stelarum with Stela, Carmen, and a gently-read Mika. At moonrise, before two factions and the emissary, Stela dissolves Mika's contract inside the salt circle while Kylie stands behind her with a hand on Mika's shoulder. Kylie says her grandmother's name aloud, formally reclaims the Marinescu line from the coven, refuses Victor's last offer on the floor of his own ballroom, and walks out at dawn — not alone. She keeps the apartment, keeps the blog, and apprentices quietly with Stela. *Dating After Dusk* triples its readership within a month and becomes the most-read English-language blog in Romania; the "Dusk Club" column lands a six-figure brand deal. Mika, free for the first time in fifty-seven years, reopens Drăgan Vintage with daytime hours. Veronica's letter is read at Stela's tea table and framed above Kylie's writing desk. Final voiceover: *Some women find their happy ending in a man. Some women find it in the mountains. I found mine in a bookshop on Lipscani at two in the morning, with two friends who finally told me the truth, and a blog that finally told me mine.*
- **Emotional register:** Triumphant-warm — the friend-group rom-com surviving everything the dark romance threw at it.
- **Theme payoff:** The full Truth and Need answered — Kylie authors her own life on her own terms, refuses all of them, frees her friend instead of being delivered by her, and proves that being adored was never the same as being seen or free.
- **State drivers:** Self-and-friends-aligned choices — hearing all of Stela's reveal at the bookshop and bringing her into confidence by Ep4, reading Mika gently and early (Branch D) and choosing to help free her in Ep7, refusing the blog wind-down and keeping the war hard-line (Branch B), refusing the dark wine (Branch C) and Victor's last ballroom offer, the quartz accepted and kept (Branch A) so the apartment held all season.
- **Target conditions:** Self-aligned pattern across at least five of the eight episode-level major choices. The quartz must be accepted and kept. Stela must have been brought into Kylie's confidence by Ep4. Mika must have been read gently and chosen for freedom. The blog must not have been wound down.
