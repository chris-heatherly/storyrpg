# StoryRPG Lite Treatment

## 1. Story Premise

- **Title:** Bite Me
- **Genre:** Paranormal rom-com that graduates into dark vampire romance.
- **Tone:** Champagne fizz on top, blood at the bottom: witty, fashionable, sexy, friend-group forward, and increasingly dangerous.
- **High concept pitch:** Sex and the City meets Twilight - dating can be a horror show in a land where the men are actual monsters.
- **Logline:** A New York food writer fleeing a publicly cancelled engagement arrives in Bucharest, starts a dating blog under codenames like Mr. Midnight and The Mountain, and slowly discovers that the man she is writing about, the friends she is writing it for, and the city itself are all monsters of one kind or another.
- **Core fantasy:** Be the new It girl in a beautiful foreign city with a friend group, a viral dating blog, a gorgeous dangerous suitor, and a quieter slow-burn alternative, while realizing that being adored is not the same as being known.
- **Themes:** Voice versus possession; adoration versus being seen; female friendship under betrayal; reinvention after public humiliation; choosing what kind of monster, if any, you become for love.
- **Audience promise:** The first three episodes feel like vacation and dating-show glamour, with a single horror thread under the champagne. The middle episodes darken into supernatural revelation, blog warfare, and romantic disillusionment. The final episodes reveal one friend after another as something other than human, forcing Kylie to decide whether she keeps her voice, her friends, her name, and her self.

## 2. Story Circle Season Spine

- **You (Ep1):** Kylie Marinescu arrives in Bucharest as a charming, wounded observer with two suitcases, her grandmother's address, and the intent to rebuild after a public breakup. She forms the Dusk Club, starts Dating After Dusk, and turns a terrifying rescue by Mr. Midnight into the first viral proof that she can author a new life.
- **Need (Ep2):** The blog's success makes Kylie visible to Victor, to anonymous watchers, and to the city beneath the city, exposing the contradiction she has not faced: she wants to be wanted, but she also needs to protect the voice that finally belongs to her.
- **Go (Ep3):** Kylie accepts Victor's invitation to Casa Stelarum and steps fully into his courtship, choosing to be the woman inside the romance rather than only the writer watching it. The Equinox weekend makes her feel lucky while planting the first concrete cracks: missing women, missing reflections, her grandmother's name, and Radu's impossible scarf.
- **Search (Ep4):** Kylie tests the alternative life Radu represents at Casa Lupului while Stela quietly strengthens the apartment's wards and Victor begins asking to discuss the blog. The season's question shifts from which man is more beautiful to which relationship, if any, can survive truth.
- **Find (Ep5):** Kylie sees Victor's truth in the mirror and runs to Stela, who reveals both Victor's strigoi nature and her own hunter lineage. The romance becomes horror, the bookshop becomes sanctuary, and Kylie finally understands that the supernatural world has been protecting and hunting her at the same time.
- **Take (Ep6):** Kylie pays for the truth by losing the last uncomplicated man in her life, learning Radu is a pricolici, seeing Carmen punished for helping her, and realizing Victor can reach through Mika even when the apartment wards hold. She refuses to surrender the blog, but the cost is open war.
- **Return (Ep7):** Kylie brings the season's accumulated truth back to the friendship that has held her together, confronting Mika and learning that her best friend is a succubus contracted to Victor. The final pressure becomes whether betrayal can be answered with freedom, cruelty, abandonment, or forgiveness.
- **Change (Ep8):** On the Hunter's Moon at Casa Stelarum, Kylie chooses what she will and will not become for love. She may become Victor's silent Consort, Radu's mortal Mountain Wife, or the Witness who keeps her friends, her blog, and her own name.

Optional:
- **Act 1:** You / Need / Go
- **Act 2:** Search / Find / Take
- **Act 3:** Return / Change

## 3. Story Arcs

### Arc: Champagne

- **Episode range:** Episodes 1-3.
- **Story Circle span:** Starts at `you`, ends at `go`, and owns `you`, `need`, and `go`.
- **Arc question:** Can a woman fleeing a publicly cancelled engagement start over, make friends, have a sex life, and let herself be wanted again in a city that does not know her ex's name?
- **Pressure movement:** Kylie moves from cautious arrival to viral blogger to willing participant in Victor's courtship, while the glamorous new life quietly reveals itself as a funnel.
- **Protagonist polarity:** Observer versus author; being chosen by a beautiful man versus claiming her own appetite and voice.
- **Key NPC/location pressure:** Victor and Casa Stelarum offer the intoxicating romance; Mika steers Kylie toward him; Stela's warnings and Radu's impossible attention imply the city is not innocent.
- **Handoff:** Victor's privacy pressure, the missing-model warning, the dark wine, the grandmother clue, and Radu's scarf make the next arc necessary because the romance can no longer stay glossy.

### Arc: Mirror

- **Episode range:** Episodes 4-6.
- **Story Circle span:** Starts at `search`, ends at `take`, and owns `search`, `find`, and `take`.
- **Arc question:** When Kylie finally lets herself want the quiet life or the dark one, what is she willing to see, and what will seeing it cost her?
- **Pressure movement:** The season shifts from flirtation and suspicion into confirmed supernatural danger, with the blog becoming the thing Victor cannot control.
- **Protagonist polarity:** Being adored versus being known; keeping the peace versus defending the public, codenamed truth of her own life.
- **Key NPC/location pressure:** Radu and Casa Lupului offer an honest alternative with its own curse, Victor escalates from charm to leverage, and Stela's bookshop becomes the truth-place.
- **Handoff:** Mika's visible seams and the black rose inside the warded apartment prove that even sanctuary has a door, making the best-friend reckoning unavoidable.

### Arc: Blood

- **Episode range:** Episodes 7-8.
- **Story Circle span:** Starts at `return`, ends at `change`, and owns `return` and `change`.
- **Arc question:** Faced with the full truth about everyone she loves and an older power forcing the matter, who is Kylie Marinescu now, and what will she and will she not become for love?
- **Pressure movement:** Kylie moves from reacting to other people's hidden designs to deciding, in public, whose hand she takes, whose freedom she protects, and whose story gets written.
- **Protagonist polarity:** Being safely adored versus claiming her voice, her friends, her grandmother's line, and her own appetite out loud.
- **Key NPC/location pressure:** Mika's contract, Stela's salt circle, Radu's return, Carmen's victim ledger, Victor's final offer, and the Strigoi Mama's emissary converge at Casa Stelarum.
- **Handoff:** The season resolves into one of three lives, while Veronica's letter, Sadie's coming visit, Stela's clan, Carmen's exposure of the coven, and the Strigoi Mama's withdrawal seed future pressure.

## 4. Protagonist Brief

- **Name and pronouns:** Kylie Marinescu, she/her.
- **Role in the world:** A 34-year-old American food writer turned blogger, quarter-Romanian on her father's side, newly arrived in Bucharest after her engagement to New York restaurateur Daniel Hayes imploded publicly.
- **Want:** Build a blog with her own name on it, have a sex life again, find friends who text back, and decide which beautiful man, if either, is worth keeping.
- **Need:** Author her own life on her own terms, claim her own appetite and voice, and learn that being adored is not the same as being known or free.
- **Lie or survival posture:** Being chosen by a beautiful man is the same as being known and safe, and her job is to observe other people's lives rather than claim her own.
- **Wound or origin pressure:** Her cancelled engagement made her feel publicly humiliated and privately unknown, and her grandmother Veronica's unexplained escape from Bucharest in 1962 left the Marinescu line unfinished.
- **Truth or possible transformation:** Her voice belongs to her, whether she falls for the honest man, refuses both men, or tragically hands that voice away.
- **Starting identity:** The observer who orders second, watches the room, writes the piece later, and second-guesses herself until the important window has nearly closed.
- **Possible end states:** The Consort, turned and owned by Victor with the blog gone dark; The Mountain Wife, mortal with Radu in the Apuseni and the blog reborn as a book; The Witness, self-authored with Mika freed, Stela beside her, and Dating After Dusk thriving; The Hollowed, a smaller failure-adjacent state if she tries to please everyone and commits to nothing.
- **Visual identity:** Honey-blonde hair she trims herself, tortoiseshell glasses for writing, slip dresses under a trench, her grandmother's gold chain, a half-charged phone, and lipstick blotted off after the first sip.

## 5. Major NPC Briefs

### NPC: Victor Valcescu ("Mr. Midnight")

- **Role:** A centuries-old strigoi, club owner, staged savior, seductive suitor, and eventual season antagonist.
- **Want:** Possess Kylie forever, turn her at Casa Stelarum, end Dating After Dusk, and keep her in the night as his dark bride.
- **Leverage:** Wealth, immortality, a hospitality empire, a country estate, media holdings, social power, and the ability to make Kylie feel like the only woman in the room.
- **Secret or contradiction:** He believes he loves Kylie, but he cannot separate love from ownership; he once intended to turn her grandmother Veronica and has staged Kylie's rescue as part of a new claim on the Marinescu line.
- **Relationship to protagonist:** The glamorous suitor whose adoration curdles into possession.
- **Voice / visual notes:** Low, even, and precise, with long pauses and compliments that feel too intimate; lean, dark-haired, silvering at the temples, in Milan-cut charcoal suits with a stag-crest signet ring and blood-dark wine.

### NPC: Radu Stoian ("The Mountain")

- **Role:** A pricolici from the Stoian pack near Bran, roadside meet-cute, honest second lead, and possible partner.
- **Want:** Live decently, avoid becoming his father, and love Kylie without lying to her, even though telling the truth may lose her.
- **Leverage:** Casa Lupului, his pack-family, practical mountain knowledge, warmth, food, and an honesty Victor cannot offer.
- **Secret or contradiction:** Once a month he is not a man, and he withholds the truth because he thinks that protects Kylie.
- **Relationship to protagonist:** The quieter life Kylie could choose, whose truth costs her as much as Victor's lies.
- **Voice / visual notes:** Careful English with mountain Romanian underneath, short sentences and long silences; broad, bearded, scarred inside the forearm, in hand-knit sweaters with woodsmoke and bay leaf in his hair.

### NPC: Mika Dragan ("The Samantha")

- **Role:** Kylie's best friend, Valescu Club door and talent power player, Dragan Vintage owner, and a contracted succubus revealed late in the season.
- **Want:** Obey Victor's contract enough to survive while being loved as herself by Kylie.
- **Leverage:** Every door in Bucharest, taste, charm, money, vintage power, and deep knowledge of the city's appetites.
- **Secret or contradiction:** She was placed in Kylie's life before Kylie arrived and has been steering her toward Victor while genuinely loving her.
- **Relationship to protagonist:** The friend Kylie trusts most, whose betrayal is devastating but potentially reconcilable.
- **Voice / visual notes:** Low-laughing, movie-quoting, Romanian/French/English code-switching, calling everyone iubita mea; platinum bob, red lips, emerald silk, leopard print, vintage gold, bracelets hiding a puncture scar, sunglasses indoors after 11am.

### NPC: Stela Pavel

- **Role:** Bookshop friend, sex-positive truth teller, Romani folk practitioner, and daughter of a strigoi-hunting clan.
- **Want:** Keep Victor's targeted women alive, atone for past loss, and live a modern queer life without betraying the old ways.
- **Leverage:** Warding, blood-warnings, herb-binding, candle and tarot divination, knowledge of strigoi killing, contract breaking, and a family that can reach Bucharest in six hours.
- **Secret or contradiction:** She has been warding Kylie's apartment through consented objects while withholding the full danger until Kylie is ready to hear it.
- **Relationship to protagonist:** The reliable truth source and the friend whose protection becomes the season's third path.
- **Voice / visual notes:** Warm, low, deliberate, emotionally honest; olive skin, long dark plait, gold hoops, linen layers, embroidered shawls, herb-stained fingers, and a quartz pendant.

### NPC: Carmen Iliescu

- **Role:** Investigative journalist, anonymous no-profile-photo warner, and later named ally.
- **Want:** Expose the Valcescu coven, publish its victims, and stop Victor before more women disappear.
- **Leverage:** A two-year victim spreadsheet, an Adevarul byline, and public credibility.
- **Secret or contradiction:** She believes she can investigate from behind anonymity until meeting Kylie makes both of them targets.
- **Relationship to protagonist:** Anonymous guardian angel turned proof that Kylie is not imagining the danger.
- **Voice / visual notes:** Clipped, factual, urgent, warmer once unmasked; deliberately unmemorable in person, later bruised and bandaged in a hospital bed while still talking to police.

## 6. World And Location Brief

- **World premise:** Modern Bucharest looks like Belle Epoque facades, Communist blocks, third-wave coffee, grandmother soups, and a club scene with teeth; underneath, strigoi run nightlife and real estate, pricolici keep to the Carpathians, Romani practitioners keep women alive, and contracted succubi feed through the dating scene.
- **Time period:** Present day, with phones, blogs, dating apps, content creators, brand deals, and modern Bucharest social life.
- **Rules that create drama:** Strigoi need invitation to enter dwellings unless a threshold is broken, cannot be photographed, and cannot directly lie about their nature when asked; pricolici inherit the curse and carry dangerous full-moon bite uncertainty; succubi are contracted and can only be freed by consented ritual before sunrise; folk practitioners can ward, warn, bind, scry, break contracts, and make rooms hostile to strigoi, but consent matters. A public codenamed blog about an unphotographable strigoi is uniquely dangerous because it gives Kylie a voice Victor cannot fully control.
- **Key locations:** Valescu Club on Calea Victoriei, Victor's seductive feeding funnel where Mika runs the door and staying too long creates danger. Cismigiu Gardens, the foggy park of the staged attack, the rescue, and the final dawn walk. Casa Lupului near Bran, Radu's warm mountain lodge and the honest alternative that asks Kylie to stay, listen, or leave. Casa Stelarum, Victor's gold boyar estate, site of the Equinox seduction and the Hunter's Moon finale. Lumina Books on Lipscani, Stela's bookshop, truth-place, warding hub, and 2am sanctuary. Kylie's Lipscani apartment, a fragile private home whose warded threshold depends on the protection Kylie accepted.

## 7. Episode Outline

### Episode 1: Dating After Dusk

- **Story Circle role:** `you`
- **High-level description:** Kylie arrives in Bucharest with two suitcases and her grandmother's address, then forms the Dusk Club with Mika and Stela over velvet booths, folklore shelves, and too-dark negronis. At a rooftop bar she catches the attention of a man in a charcoal suit and a rougher man near the kitchen. Walking home through Cismigiu, she is attacked and rescued by the impossibly handsome stranger, who walks her to her threshold and vanishes. At 4am she turns the night into the first Dating After Dusk post under the codename Mr. Midnight, and by evening the post has gone viral.
- **Major pressure:** Can Kylie start over, feel wanted, and write under her own name in a city that is already watching her?
- **Likely consequence:** The blog, Dusk Club, Victor's staged courtship, Stela's protection, Mika's placement, Radu's first sighting, and Kylie's first authored act all become live season anchors.

### Episode 2: Mr. Midnight

- **Story Circle role:** `need`
- **High-level description:** Dating After Dusk becomes a real local curiosity as Kylie turns bad dates into material, gathers attention, and follows Mika's repeated nudges toward Valescu Club. Victor gives her the best conversation she has had in years, praising her writing while remaining too private and too informed. On a mountain research trip, Kylie's cab breaks down and Radu fixes it, feeds her bread, and gives her his number. She returns home with two very different men in her phone, a new codename for The Mountain, and an anonymous warning that a woman vanished at Victor's last party.
- **Major pressure:** Who is Mr. Midnight, and how brave is Kylie willing to be about finding out when both desire and warning point at him?
- **Likely consequence:** Kylie is no longer merely writing about strangers; the blog's reach, Victor's attention, Radu's alternative, and the first outside warning push her toward an irreversible courtship.

### Episode 3: The Weekend

- **Story Circle role:** `go`
- **High-level description:** Victor invites the Dusk Club to Casa Stelarum for the Equinox weekend, and Kylie lets herself be golden, courted, and kissed in the hedge maze at midnight. Beneath the romance she notices a crying model, a strange photograph, her grandmother's maiden name spoken too casually, and an hour Mika cannot account for. Victor gently frames the blog as a privacy problem, asking for her discretion without yet sounding like a threat. Kylie returns to Bucharest feeling lucky, then finds Radu's hand-knit scarf on a doorstep whose address she never gave him.
- **Major pressure:** What is Kylie willing not to see in order to keep the feeling of being beautifully chosen?
- **Likely consequence:** Kylie's desire for Victor becomes real, but the blog-versus-privacy conflict, Veronica thread, missing-woman danger, dark-wine temptation, and Radu's impossible knowledge turn the romance unstable.

### Episode 4: The Mountain

- **Story Circle role:** `search`
- **High-level description:** Kylie drives to Casa Lupului with Radu's scarf beside her and lets the mountain lodge offer a quieter life of woodsmoke, food, cousins, and almost-honest tenderness. Radu asks if she has ever felt like two different women, then pulls back from an almost-kiss because of a truth he cannot yet say. In Bucharest, Stela deepens the apartment's warding through a new-moon candle while Mika keeps warning Kylie away from the mountain. The night ends with a warning that Ileana is alive and changed, plus Victor's polite email asking to discuss the blog.
- **Major pressure:** Is Radu's quiet life a refuge from Victor, or just a different curse Kylie has not learned to read?
- **Likely consequence:** The season gains a genuine second romantic pole, the apartment's protection becomes crucial, Victor's pressure on the blog becomes explicit, and Radu's withheld past drives the next revelation.

### Episode 5: Mirror, Mirror

- **Story Circle role:** `find`
- **High-level description:** Victor asks Kylie to wind down Dating After Dusk in exchange for private opportunities that sound generous and feel like surrender. That night she goes to his apartment, and the romance turns inside out when the mirror shows her standing alone. Kylie runs barefoot to Lumina Books, where Stela tells her Victor is a strigoi and that Stela is Romani from a hunter line that has been warding her all along. At sunrise Kylie skips the blog for the first time and sees Victor's stag-crest ring in her grandmother Veronica's 1961 photograph.
- **Major pressure:** What does Kylie do when the man she wanted is revealed as the monster, and the friend she underestimated is revealed as the protector?
- **Likely consequence:** The genre flips, Victor's nature and Stela's truth become canonical, Kylie's lie about adoration breaks, and the Marinescu inheritance thread becomes active.

### Episode 6: Wolf at the Door

- **Story Circle role:** `take`
- **High-level description:** Kylie processes the mirror with Stela, then goes to Casa Lupului to ask Radu about 2015. Radu confesses he is a pricolici, tells the truth about his family and his scars, and Kylie walks out with another beautiful man changed by honesty instead of lies. The anonymous warner reveals herself as Carmen Iliescu, but Carmen is beaten before the meeting and Radu is crudely framed as Victor's message. Kylie publishes Don't Tell Me What to Write and finds a black rose inside the warded apartment, delivered by Mika because Victor asked her to.
- **Major pressure:** Is anyone in Kylie's life just a person, and can she defend her voice when every truth costs another relationship?
- **Likely consequence:** The blog war becomes open, Radu's truth is paid for, Carmen becomes a named ally and victim, and Mika's role as Victor's hand forces the next episode's confrontation.

### Episode 7: Best Friend

- **Story Circle role:** `return`
- **High-level description:** Stela lays out the case against Mika over tea, and Kylie spends the day rereading every text for seams. At midnight in the locked Dragan Vintage, Mika confesses she is a succubus bound to Victor since 1968, placed in Kylie's life before Kylie arrived, and still genuinely in love with her as a friend. Mika asks Kylie what to do, leaving Kylie to decide whether the betrayal can lead to freedom. Stela offers a Hunter's Moon salt-circle release, Carmen's spreadsheet waits as a public weapon, and Radu returns beneath Kylie's balcony admitting he tried to protect her the first night.
- **Major pressure:** Can Kylie hold on to the friend she loves most after learning that the friendship began as an assignment?
- **Likely consequence:** Mika's truth becomes the finale's emotional center, Kylie holds the choices that will determine the Hunter's Moon, and every faction is now pointed at Casa Stelarum.

### Episode 8: Bite Me

- **Story Circle role:** `change`
- **High-level description:** On the Hunter's Moon, Carmen's victim spreadsheet goes live and the Valcescu Coven gathers at Casa Stelarum as an emissary of the Strigoi Mama arrives to settle the Marinescu line. Kylie walks in aligned with Victor, Radu, or Stela and Mika, and the ballroom turns the season's entire conflict into a public choice about her name, her voice, Mika's freedom, and her future. Victor asks one last time for the blog and her hand, Radu offers the honest mortal life, and Stela's salt circle offers a third path. At dawn Kylie exits into one of three lives, with Veronica's letter still surfacing no matter what she chooses.
- **Major pressure:** Who is Kylie Marinescu now, and what will she and will she not become for love?
- **Likely consequence:** The season resolves into Consort, Mountain Wife, or Witness, while Veronica's letter, Sadie's visit, Stela's clan, Carmen's exposure of the coven, and the Strigoi Mama remain future pressure.

## 8. Alternate Endings

### Ending 1: The Consort

Kylie accepts Victor's Hunter's Moon offer, agrees to wind down Dating After Dusk, and becomes his dark bride in a tragic-glamorous ending that looks like ascension and lands as annihilation. Mika remains bound or unreleased, Stela is kept outside the gate, and Veronica's letter goes unread into a drawer. This ending pays off repeated Victor-aligned choices: choosing courtship over voice, drinking the dark wine, softening the blog, reading Mika cruelly or not at all, and leaving the apartment's protection weak. The thematic meaning is that the Lie wins beautifully: being chosen by a beautiful man is mistaken one last time for being known and safe.

### Ending 2: The Mountain Wife

Kylie chooses Radu, helps Carmen expose the coven, refuses Victor in his own ballroom, and leaves Bucharest at dawn for a mortal life at Casa Lupului. Dating After Dusk becomes a book that carries her name, Mika can be freed, and Kylie keeps her voice by turning the public blog into a quieter but lasting work. This ending pays off repeated Radu-aligned choices: choosing honesty over glamour, staying with the mountain truth, defending the blog when Victor frames Radu, refusing the dark wine, and keeping the apartment's protection intact. The thematic meaning is romantic but difficult: the bravest thing a heartbroken woman can do may be to fall again for someone who tells the truth.

### Ending 3: The Witness

Kylie chooses neither man and walks into Casa Stelarum with Stela, Carmen, and a gently read Mika, then stands behind Mika at the salt circle as the contract is dissolved. She claims the Marinescu line, refuses Victor's last offer, keeps the apartment, keeps the blog, and apprentices quietly with Stela while Dating After Dusk becomes more powerful than ever. This ending pays off repeated self-and-friends-aligned choices: trusting Stela, reading Mika with compassion, refusing the blog wind-down, rejecting the dark wine, and protecting the warded sanctuary. The thematic meaning is the full answer to Kylie's Need: her voice belongs to her, and being adored was never the same as being seen or free.
